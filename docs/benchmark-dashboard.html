Local models fail at tool-calling.
A 1 kLOC harness fixes most of it — measured.

> Same model, same tasks, five harnesses — plus neutral third-party tasks from the Berkeley Function Calling Leaderboard. A minimal TypeScript harness (prompt protocol, single-call turns, error feedback) lifts llama3.1 from 56% to 92% task success (official k=5 run, 250 runs per cell), beats Hugging Face smolagents off-the-shelf on weak models, and holds its edge on data it never saw. Every number below carries its confidence interval and its counter-evidence.

Limitations, stated plainly

The in-house suite was designed by the harness author and the harness was debugged against it — it therefore carries the uplift claim only (all harnesses run identical tasks, models and seeds), never a "best harness" claim. That is what the BFCL numbers are for.

Rival numbers are off-the-shelf defaults: they measure what a user gets out of the box, not the ceiling of those libraries. On the stronger qwen3:8b the picture narrows: +8 pp in-house (not significant) and parity on BFCL (83% vs 83%) — the harness pays off most where models are weakest. Charts marked probe are single-seed runs (n=50/bar); official k=5 numbers live in BENCHMARKS.md, the full fairness protocol and equal-effort log in bench/FAIRNESS.md.
